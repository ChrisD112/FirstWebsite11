The Stanley Cup

The Stanley Cup is the championship trophy awarded annually to the winner of the National Hockey League. After an 82-game regular season, sixteen teams enter the playoffs to compete for a chance to win it. The playoffs consist of four intense rounds, each decided in a best-of-seven series. The team that survives all four rounds earns the right to hoist the Stanley Cup. As the oldest trophy in North American professional sports, first awarded in 1893, it remains one of the most iconic symbols in all of hockey. The 2018 Capitals

The 2018 Washington Capitals had a historic season that ended with the franchise first-ever Stanley Cup championship. Led by captain Alex Ovechkin, the team showed resilience after years of playoff disappointment. They defeated the Columbus Blue Jackets, Pittsburgh Penguins, and Tampa Bay Lightning to reach the Stanley Cup Final. In the Final, they overcame the Vegas Golden Knights in five games, securing their long-awaited victory. The 2018 Capitals are remembered for their determination, teamwork, and the unforgettable celebration that followed their championship win. 2018 Info

Capitals Awards

Several Washington Capitals players have earned major NHL awards throughout the team's history. Alex Ovechkin has led the way, winning multiple Hart Trophies as league MVP, as well as the Rocket Richard Trophy numerous times as the NHL's top goal-scorer. Nicklas Backstrom has been recognized for his playmaking excellence, including earning the Lady Byng Memorial Trophy for sportsmanship and skill. Goaltender Braden Holtby captured the Vezina Trophy in 2016 as the league's top goalie after an outstanding season. These award-winning players have helped shape the Capitals' identity and contributed to the franchise's long-term success.
