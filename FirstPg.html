The Washington Capitals

Who Are the Washington Capitals?

The Washington Capitals are a professional hockey team that plays in the National Hockey League. They play in the Metropolitan Division in the Eastern Conference, which formed in 2013 as a result in league realignment. It is the only Divison in the NHL that does not have a Canadian team in it. They play at the Capital One Arena in Washington, D.C. Capital One Arena has a maximum capacity of 18,573, and is also home to the Washington Wizards. The Capitals were founded in 1974 as an expansion team.

Famous Players

There have been many popular Capitals players to play for the expansion franchise. In all time leading goal scorer Alex Ovechkin, John Carlson, and Ryan Strome are all current Capitals who are often said has best players on the team, for their skill and leadership. As of right now, Alex Ovechkin is the 3rd highest jersey sold on NHL.com Jersey Sells. In terms of all time, names like Alex Ovechkin, Nicklas Backstrom, TJ Oshie, Braden Holtby, and Dale Hunter are frequent names brought up and often talked about as some of the best Capitals ever.

Records

The Capitals have an overall record of 1880-1591-303. The best season in the franchise's history was the 2009-2010 season. They had a record of 54-15-13 finishing first in their division with 121 points and winning the Presidents' Trophy. The Presidents' Trophy is awarded to the top team in the league, who accumulates the most points from wins and overtime losses. Player point wise, the Capitals all-time record for points is Alex Ovechkin with 1,643. Dennis Maruk holds the Capitals record for most points in a single season, with 136.
